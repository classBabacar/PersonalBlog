Connect4

Growing up I played this game a lot, it means so much to me because of the friendships I've developed playing it. Eventually, when I increased my programming skills this was one of the programs I wanted to create the most and learned so much from making this.

Connect4 is a simple game to the naked eye but as you begin to study it you notice it's complexities, this was very rewarding theses were the following concepts I learned:

  • Classes - A way to decompose a game like connect4 into pieces, color and board.
  • Minimax - A way for an AI to evaluate a sequence of board states and make a logical decision.
  • Operating Systems/Docker - It was cool to figure out the opcodes of different colors on different machines it made me see the bigger picture not even for games but when I write any program in general, try to give everyone the oppurtunty to view/use it.

For more information you can find it here: Connect4

Mastermind

As I got a better grasp on algorithms, my interest was piqued which resulted in me coming upon something very interesting. In 1977, Donald Knuth came up with a really cool idea for this game called mastermind. In simple explanation, he explained through mathematics that the game can be won in 5 moves or less where players are given a maximum of 11 moves.

Most of the time for me words can't do justice, that's what drove me to start this program. It also noteworthy that most of the "great" programmers made algorithmic breakthroughs through games such as: Game of Life, Minesweeper, Chess, and soo many more.

For more information you can find it here: Mastermind
